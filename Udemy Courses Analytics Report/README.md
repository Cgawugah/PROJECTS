**Udemy Courses Analytics Dashboard**

This project presents a full Power BI analysis of Udemy course performance, focusing on subscribers, revenue trends, subject popularity, publication activity, and user engagement. Using a robust dataset that includes both free and paid courses, the dashboard offers a comprehensive view of Udemy’s marketplace dynamics and highlights the patterns that shape online learning demand.

**Project Overview**

The objective of this project is to explore how course subjects, instructor choices, publication years, and course levels influence performance metrics such as revenue, number of subscribers, reviews, and overall marketplace growth.
The dashboard is designed for stakeholders interested in understanding what drives success on Udemy and how content creation trends evolve over time.

**Key Insights and Metrics**

**The report covers several analytical perspectives:**

Global KPIs

12M total subscribers

574K total reviews

147K total lectures

882M total revenue

Popular Courses

Visuals identify the top 5 courses based on the number of subscribers. The report uses pie charts and treemaps to highlight which courses dominate user interest and how they compare to others.

**Preferred Subjects**

A detailed comparison of free vs paid courses within each subject category reveals which subjects attract the highest number of subscribers.
Findings show that Web Development leads significantly, followed by Business Finance, Graphic Design, and Musical Instruments.

**Publication and Revenue Trends**

The dashboard analyzes historical data spanning from 2011 onward to show:

How course creation has changed over time

How revenue has grown or declined across the years

The relationship between course volume and monetization

Line charts and dual-axis visuals help reveal patterns in course publication and financial performance.

**Subject-wise Revenue Contribution**

A breakdown of total revenue by subject shows a heavy concentration in Web Development, followed by smaller but notable contributions from Business Finance, Graphic Design, and Musical Instruments.

Subscribers by Course Level

A donut chart displays the distribution of subscribers among

All Levels

Beginner

Intermediate

Expert
This helps identify which difficulty levels attract the most learners.

**Reviews by Subject**

A comparison of review counts across subjects gives insight into user engagement and satisfaction levels associated with various topic areas.

**Final Dashboard**

All visuals are consolidated into a single interactive summary page that provides:

Filters for subject and course level

Top courses

Trends

Revenue breakdown

Engagement indicators

This summary page allows users to quickly explore marketplace behavior and make data-driven decisions.
